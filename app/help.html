    • Executing your R-Script

    A detailed explanation on how to execute a .R file from your "Scripts" folder

     

    After creating your script and filling it with your favorite R-code, why not run it? 😉

    Move all your attention to that little tree-view on the left side of the screen.

    Look for your "Scripts" directory. That's the place where all your work is stored!

    The hardest part is done! Simply select the soon-to-be-executed script by clicking on it in the tree-view and press the "RUN selected Script!" button.

    Creating your own TMS

    Now that you are able to execute your R-scripts, why dont we put your results onto the map? 😎

     

    To be able to put your result onto the map, we need to create a TMS.

    Easy peasy lemon squeezy!

    For that purpose we advice you to use a function called as_PNG_layer


    Here is a little example for you:

    firstimage = slice(x = scidbst("SENTINEL2_MS"), "t", 0)
    as_PNG_layer(firstimage,TMS = TRUE, bands = 4,
    layername="S2_NIR_T0",
    min=300, max=5000, rm.scidb = TRUE)

  • SENTINEL2_MS is a file we have in our db
  • Creates an image "S2_NIR-T0.png"
  • The value 5000 will be depicted to white
  • The value 300 will be depicted to black
  • After that, the subfolder "TMS/S2_NIR_T0" will have all the tiles for the map and a leaflet html example!

    • If you were to look right below your tree-view, you can see an input field. Please put the path to your TMS-tiles folder in there and press the "Load TMS from path" button

    Congratz! Your TMS is loaded into the map and can be seen by clicking it in the layercontrol (top right of the map)😉

    Note:

    You can choose the layers that you want to see in your TMS via the bands attribute.
